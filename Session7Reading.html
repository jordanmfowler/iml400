The Verge, The Rise and Fall of Virtual Reality starts off by reminding us about the tremendous escape power of virtual reality: "The promise of virtual reality has always been enormous. Put on these goggles, go nowhere, and be transported anywhere.”  
 
I think it’s weird how this article thinks of VR as being organic. I hope that even as this field develops, we can and need to separate how these artificial created experiences will never be able to truly replicate reality. VR should be reserved for specific types of explorations, because it will never be able to truly re-create several aspects of life. I think the article addresses this point by saying: "That’s the promise VR has today. VR at its best shouldn’t replace real life, just modify it, giving us access to so much just out of reach physically, economically.” The places I see VR on the right track include the examples where it is helping train lazy eyes or when it is used to explore places in the world that we don’t have access to. 
 
This article does a good job explaining how time is everything when it comes to the success or development of a new project or idea. VR wasn’t popular in the 90’s because the internet was being developed at the time so it’s innovation trumped VR. Now, VR is the thing.
 
It was interesting to learn about why Facebook was interested in such a device. This justification helped me learn more about the affordances of VR in terms of how people can use the device to help share experiences. On this note, it was interesting to learn about why Facebook finds VR appealing; Facebook believes that VR can take advantage of our connection and familiarity with spatial environments. 
 
In the article, "Virtual Reality Comes to the Web—Maybe for Real This Time Backed by Google and Mozilla, VR-enabled browsers and gear could soon immerse Web users in 3-D worlds” they note how Google released a browser friendly VR set in 2015, but I haven’t heard about it.
 
As a result, in conclusion I think VR definitely is a slow rollout and to be honest, it is a type of technology that I don’t think at this point is useful in many instances so learning where and when it is applicable instead of just through new tech at existing systems will be the key to successful development.
