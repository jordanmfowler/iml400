In "The Internet as Art: In the digital age, the medium is the new message” I was able to get re-familiarzed with technology as being a form for art to be expressed. 

I think we need to come to a point in our society where we have a deeper appreciation for art. I think art is unique because it is a medium where anything goes. It is personal and specific, broad and distant all at the same time. Art is a powerful medium to talk about when it comes to technology, because it brings the human element back into a world that seems so driven by forces out of ones control. 

Reading about Symantec’s anti virus software that stopped the spread of biennale.py in 2001 had an interesting angle that emphasizes how computers don’t spread viruses, but instead how humans do. I appreciate the emphasis this reading has on the cycle of human-machine-human because often times we so easily forget that humans start and end cycles with technology and as much as we are dependent on the tech, the tech is dependent on us.

This article touches upon ideas related interdisciplinary multimedia and transmedia. When discussing the work “tv screen, night one” the reading says: “'It was a piece about the relation between TV and the Web,' Mr. Cosic says. 'At the time I was insisting on the fact that when it comes to global coverage, TV was still in advantage in terms of reach and credibility over the Internet.’” I think today there is an emphasis on being able to take advantage of technology and use the affordances of the different platforms to create multimedia projects that go beyond 1D projects - this is something I hope to do in all of my projects!
